# IXDS 709 — Assignment 1: Case Study Research Prompt

---

I'm doing a case study for a graduate design class called **Systems Innovation: Beyond Human-Centered Design (IXDS 709)**. The assignment requires me to find a real-world product or project that **moved beyond user-centered design** — meaning it didn't just optimize the user experience or interface, but considered the broader system (supply chains, environmental impact, social infrastructure, policy, material science, public health, etc.) to address a root cause.

**Here's what qualifies:** The project must be something that was actually produced (not conceptual). The designers had to be thinking at the *systems level* — not just "how do we make this easier to use," but "why does this system work this way, and what if we redesigned the underlying structure?" A good example: **NOBS Toothpaste Tablets** — they didn't redesign the toothpaste tube, they questioned why oral care ships water in plastic tubes at all, and redesigned the entire product system (tablet form, no preservatives needed, zero-waste packaging, biomimetic nano-hydroxyapatite instead of fluoride, TSA-compliant, pre-portioned dosing).

I need a **diverse list of 10–15 candidates** across different domains. For each, give me a 2–3 sentence summary of what it is and why it qualifies as beyond-UCD / systems-level thinking. Here are the domains I want covered — at least one strong candidate per domain:

1. **Consumer products / packaging** (e.g., products that rethought the material or distribution system, not just the label)
2. **Electronics / hardware** (e.g., Fairphone's modular, ethical-supply-chain smartphone — conflict-free minerals from DRC, user-repairable, transparent supply chain mapping)
3. **Urban infrastructure / transportation** (e.g., Bogotá's Ciclovía + TransMilenio system — 100km of car-free streets every Sunday integrated with bus rapid transit, redesigning urban mobility rather than just improving traffic signals)
4. **Food systems / agriculture** (e.g., Too Good To Go food waste app — but note the UC Berkeley study showing it may encourage overproduction, so look for stronger examples)
5. **Healthcare / public health** (products or systems that addressed health at the infrastructure level, not just the patient interface)
6. **Education / digital equity** (projects that addressed root causes of educational inequality, not just built another app)
7. **Fashion / textiles** (circular economy, ethical labor, systemic redesign of how clothing is made/consumed)
8. **Housing / architecture** (participatory design, community-driven development, systemic approaches to housing)
9. **Energy / environment** (designed systems, not just policy — something with a tangible designed artifact)
10. **Finance / economic systems** (designed products or platforms that rethought how money/resources flow to underserved populations)

For each candidate, tell me: what it is, who made it, when it launched, and in 2–3 sentences why it qualifies as systems-level thinking beyond UCD. Flag which ones have the richest material available for a visual PDF case study (images, diagrams, before/after comparisons). I want to walk into class with options, not just one idea.
