# Memory Aids — Half a Good House

**S1: COVER** [30s]
- Three half-houses, delivered like this on purpose
- $7,500 of government money, Iquique, 2004
- Right answer to the wrong question

**S2: THE PROBLEM** [90s]
- Chile wasn't failing — it was the *model*
- 100K units/year, 1.7M homes in 30 years
- $7,500 = land + infrastructure + house → 36 sqm box on the periphery
- "Los con techo" — the roof *is* the problem now

**S3: CONTEXT** [90s]
- Feedback loop: periphery → depreciation → 70% delinquency → 2-3hr commutes → shattered networks
- Iquique: 100 families, Quinta Monroy, since the 1960s
- 2000: landowner dies, land triples, families face eviction
- They refused to leave

**S4: FEEDBACK LOOP** [45s]
- Walk the loop: cheap land → isolation → depreciation → no equity → stays poor → cheap land again
- Not a *housing* problem — a *location* problem
- Elemental broke it at the root: kept them on central land, 3x the cost

**S5: THE VOID** [20s]
- Let the image breathe
- Everything they *can't* build is here. Everything they *can* is missing. On purpose.

**S6: DESIGN** [90s]
- The reframe: "What if 36 sqm is *half* of a good house?"
- Built the "difficult half" — concrete, foundation, roof, kitchen, bath, stairs
- Left expansion void — "middle-class DNA"
- 34 ground-floor (→70 sqm) + 59 duplexes (→72 sqm), four courtyards

**S7: ARCHITECTURAL EVIDENCE** [30s]
- Floor plan: expansion zones labeled "futura ampliación"
- Elevation: both typologies stacked
- Before/after interiors: same bones, different life

**S8: STATS** [20s]
- 93 families. $7,500/unit. 36→72 sqm. 230% property value increase.

**S9: THE SYSTEM** [15s]
- Courtyard as delivered — raw concrete, open voids
- The system *before* residents transformed it

**S10: SYSTEMS — BEYOND HCD** [90s]
- UCD would have made a *better* 36 sqm box — that's optimizing the product
- Aravena changed four systems:
  1. Policy — subsidy buys half a unit (unprecedented)
  2. Land economics — 70% of budget on location
  3. Construction — designed for 72 sqm end state
  4. Resident role — co-builder, sweat equity → financial equity
- Quote: "Nothing worse than answering the wrong question well"

**S11: PARTICIPATORY DESIGN** [60s]
- Residents threatened a hunger strike over apartments
- Apartments can't expand — that rejection *produced* the half-house
- Key question: "Which half do you need us to build?"
- Design Justice: "Nothing about us without us" — the strike was the most important data point

**S12: BEFORE/AFTER** [45s]
- Within 1 year: values tripled ($7,500 → $20,000+)
- 12 years: 92 of 93 expanded
- Rental apartments, ground-floor businesses, proximity to markets
- House became an investment vehicle, not a depreciating box

**S13: IMPACT** [45s]
- Reversed feedback loop: central location → access → expansion → value → wealth
- "Tool to overcome poverty" not "shelter against the environment"
- But: uneven construction quality, low-quality materials replicate informal conditions
- 31% encroached on courtyards. Participation ended at handover. No governance.
- Global scale: hasn't happened. Aravena: "still a failure"

**S14: VILLA VERDE / CONSTITUCIÓN** [60s]
- Feb 27, 2010: 8.8 earthquake, tsunami, 80% of buildings ruined
- Elemental hired: 484 houses + city master plan + 16-hectare tsunami forest
- Scaling proof: 93 families → 46,000 people, same method

**S15: TIMELINE** [30s]
- 2004 Quinta Monroy → 2008 Venice → 2010 earthquake → 2016 Pritzker
- Open-sourced designs: 40K downloads year one
- 2025 Aravena: "It's still a failure. The mainstream has not been affected."

**S16: CRITICISM** [75s — don't rush]
1. **Skill gap** — many additions used slum-quality materials, 31% encroached on courtyards
2. **Ideological** — Boano & Vergara: "Why should the poor receive a half-house instead of a proper one?" Normalizes inadequate provision
3. **Participation ended at handover** — no ongoing support, no community governance
4. **Scalability** — despite everything, the dominant model hasn't changed

**S17: PEOPLE + CHANGE** [60s]
- Concentric impact: 93 families → officials → 2,500 in later projects → 40K downloads → profession
- Project level: yes. City level: yes. Global: not yet.
- The system bent. It didn't break. That's the systems story.

## Q&A Triggers

- **"More money?"** → Subsidy increases get eaten by land inflation. Pragmatic vs. ideological.
- **"Gentrification?"** → Values tripled but families stayed. Structural risk is real, hasn't materialized yet.
- **"Design Justice?"** → Hunger strike = real authority. But participation ended at handover. Partial implementation.
- **"Work in the US?"** → Aravena is skeptical. Chile's unique: subsidy system, housing-as-right culture, self-build tradition.
- **"vs. Habitat?"** → Habitat designs for delivery moment. Elemental designs for the trajectory (72 sqm end state). Location decision is the systems difference.
